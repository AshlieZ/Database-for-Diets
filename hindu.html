Hindu Diet

Diet:

  • No beef
  • May follow a vegetarian diet depending on the person

About:

To Hindus, cows are considered a holy animal which is why beef is prohibited from their diets. Hinduism focuses on the belief of reincarnation and that every living being has a soul, making them more reluctant to kill a living creature.

But because Hinduism is a diverse religion, food practices vary. While some may only abstain from eating beef, others may be lacto-vegetarian, meaning that they do not eat both meat and eggs, although dairy products are fine.
