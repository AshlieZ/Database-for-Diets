Kosher

Diet:

  • Restrictions on meat, common meats that are not allowed are shellfish and pork
  • Cannot eat meat with diary in the same meal
  • Meat must be prepared kosher

About:

There is a portion of Jews who follow the kashrut, Jewish dietary laws. The food that these laws allow are called kosher. These are the rules for allowed animals (meat):

  • Land animals must have split hooves and must eat grass
  • Seafood must have fins and scales, so shellfish is not allowed
  • It is forbidden to eat birds of prey, only birds that do not eat other animals, so oultry is allowed
  • Meat and dairy cannot be eaten together

Animals must also be slaughtered in a proper way by a shochet. Shochets are liscensed and trained to slaughter animals following the kashrut. Blood must also be drained and animals that have died natural deaths are forbidden from being eaten. Eating a kosher diet varies in the Jewish population; some choose to follow it while others do not. A safe option for those who do eat kosher is to eat vegetarian outside, because the kashrut only has restrictions on meat.
