Gluten-Free

Diet:

  • No gluten, meaning any ingredients made of wheat, barley, or rye like pasta, cereal, or beer

About:

There are many reasons why a person may have to eat a gluten-free diet. One is celiac disease. Celiac disease is where a person’s body sees gluten as a virus and tries to fight it. As a result, their body damages the small intestine causing inflammation and digestion problems. There are also people with wheat allergies who follow a gluten-free lifestyle. They experience the same variety of symptoms as any other allergy. If a person doesn’t fall into any of these categories and when taking gluten out of their diets they stop feeling symptoms (symptoms are similar to celiac disease), they are diagnosed with having non-celiac gluten sensitivity. The cause for this condition is still being researched.

Some grains that are gluten-free alternatives are rice, quinoa, corn, tapioca, millet, amaranth, buckwheat, sorghum, potatoes, yucca, plantain, beans, lentils, and chickpeas.
