Lactose Intolerance

Diet:

  • No milk, other dairy products may vary from person to person
  • Symptoms can be abated by taking lactase supplements or other medications

About:

Lactose is the main sugar component found in milk. When ingesting milk, our body uses proteins called lactase to digest it. Those who are lactose intolerant do not produce enough lactase in their small intestine to break down lactose, and end up experiencing digestive-related symptoms, such as bloating, abdominal pain, gas, and diarrhea. Lactose intolerance is a hereditary trait, and most people who are lactose intolerant are born with it.

People who are lactose intolerant generally abstain from milk and milk products to avoid the discomfort of the symptoms. However, they are not life-threatening, and some do choose to keep milk in their eating habits, just lessening the amount. Also, different milk products have different levels of lactose. Things like yogurt or cheez-its have low levels and may not even cause symptoms. There are also supplements like Lactaid that contain lactase to help lactose intolerants break down lactose after ingesting it.
